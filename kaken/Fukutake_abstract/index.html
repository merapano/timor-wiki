Wars and Migrations in Southern Tetum Society, Timor Island

Abstract

This paper demonstrates the importance of the kingdom of Wehali for the Timorese, especially those who lived in the western part of Timor-Leste, during past wars. People who lived in the western part of Timor-Leste such as Cova Lima, Bobonaro, and Ermera have fled to the villages of their relatives in Belu, West Timor. For outsiders, this evacuation seems to be the act of ‘refugees’ who flee beyond national borders. However, this meant that people fled to the ‘centre’ of the ancient kingdom, particularly because the elders had transferred the luliks, or ancestral objects, there. Luliks are passed down from one generation to the next and can be in the form of various objects, such as local antique textiles called tais, ceramics obtained from Chinese merchants, and even the Holy Cross and Bible given by Catholic missionaries.

Focusing on the movement of people and luliks during the past wars, I have pointed out that the centre of the ancient kingdom continues to be important, particularly for people who live in the western part of the new nation state, and that there exists a cultural identity beyond the national border in central Timor.
