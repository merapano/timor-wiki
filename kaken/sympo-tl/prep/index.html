奥田：Eu entendo português（ポルトガル語、わかります）が意味するもの

東ティモールでは、ポルトガル統治下、インドネシ ア時代、独立後のぞれぞれの時期において教育言語 や仕事言語に変更があったために、世代ごとの習得 言語が異なっている。本発表ではまず、実用語であ るインドネシア語、公用語ポルトガル語およびテトゥ ン語の現況をまとめる。若年層のインドネシア語離 れが予想以上のペースで進んでいる。ポルトガル語 に関しては、若者や大学生は、会話能力が低い一方 で読解能力が高い傾向がある。テトゥン語は、今後 は表記のゆれの解消が公用語として欠かせない。発 表では最後に、ポルトガル語が世代間の対立（他者 イメージ）の要因になっている点を具体例とともに 指摘する。

福武：Refugees and the Cross

  • Full title: Refugees and the Cross: Religion, Languages, and Borderland in Timor

During the 1999 East Timorese crisis, many people moved from East Timor to Indonesian Timor. The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) and international NGOs organized humanitarian aid for the refugees, recognizing the refugees as hostages of local militias and the Indonesian military. This act antagonized the militias against the UNHCR and the NGOs that ultimately led to a tragedy.

I would like to analyzes these events from the perspective of religion, local languages, and cross-border issues in East Timor, which have been prevalent since its colonization. Central Timor, an area along the border between East Timor and Indonesia, is home to a matrilineal society. The society originated under the Wehali kingdom, whose center was in south central Timor. People living in that area speak Tetun, an official language of East Timor, and the majority is Catholic with their own history and culture. During the war, people from East Timor migrated and brought along a sacred cross to a village in West Timor as a means of protection. They took shelter in their ancestral village in West Timor because the place was the ritual center for Timorese people. This argues that the people migrated to West Timor not to take refuge “beyond the border” but to seek sanctuary. At that time, international aid providers' misunderstandings about local people triggered disorder along the border.
