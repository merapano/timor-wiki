２つのティモール・レステ

２つの「ティモール・レステ」がある。

一つは21世紀初の独立国家として、 1975年以来苦難の歴史をもつティモール・レステ である。

もう一つは村で、あるいは町で生きる 人々の日常生活である。

この小さな国の独立は、幾多の苦難をのりこえて 達成された。・・・五世紀にもおよぶ外国支配の 下で東ティモール人は自由となる日を夢みてきた (i)

traube-review jolliffe が書くように、

This reviewer, unlikeJ olliffe, is an anthropologist who has spent two years on East Timor. What is distressing about Jolliffe's book to an ethnographer --- for theoretical reasons, not from professional hauteur --- is the way in which the Timorese people are allowed to exist solely in reactiont o colonialism and nationalism, as if these two processes somehow constituted Timorese reality. The Timorese are portrayed as passive figures, exploited by the Portuguese, rallied by FRETILIN, manipulated by pro-Indonesian forces. Things happen to the Timorese, who function as active subjects only in a negative capacity, clinging to the troublesome "tribalism" which hinders national unity --- and which, for Jolliffe, is itself the product of colonialism. (pp 447--448)

"Tribal rivalry" is no explanation of the historicalr eality in which Timorese have been known to hate, kill, ignore,r espect, assist, exploit, manipulate, despise, tolerate, and cooperate with one another. Profound economic, social, political, and cultural differences exist on East Timor which can both divide and unite the population. Only a naive nationalism would seek to ignore this diversity, and a study of Timorese nationalism must confront it as a positive phenomenon. Such a study must approach the Timorese people not as objects responding to colonialist oppressorors nationalist liberators, but as active subjects who, as members of a determinate social formation, perceive and pursue their own interests.
